Open Sources

FreeCodeCamp (Freecodecamp) Offers a comprehensive curriculum covering HTML, CSS, JavaScript, Node.js, Express.js, MongoDB, React, and more. It includes interactive lessons and projects.



The Odin Project (Odin Project) A free and open-source curriculum for learning full stack web development. It covers HTML, CSS, JavaScript, Node.js, Express.js, and SQL.



Coursera (coursera) Offers courses from universities and organizations worldwide, some of which are free to audit. Courses cover full stack development, including front-end and back-end technologies.



Codecademy (Codecademy) Offers interactive courses on full stack technologies such as HTML, CSS, JavaScript, Python, SQL, and more. Some content is free, while a Pro subscription gives access to additional features.



Stack Overflow (stackoverflow) A Q&A platform where you can find solutions to specific problems in full stack development. It's a valuable resource for troubleshooting and learning from others' challenges.



YouTube (YouTube) Many developers and organizations share tutorials and lectures on full stack development. Channels like Traversy Media, The Net Ninja, and freeCodeCamp.org offer valuable content.
